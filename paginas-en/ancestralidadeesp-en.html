Spiritual Ancestry: Religions, heritage and tradition

The Brazilian Caboclos, as spiritual entities present in the various manifestations religious spiritualists existing in Brazil, whose roots are religiosity indigenous and African, are the inheritance and permanence of indigenous ancestry and tradition among the Afro-based religions in Brazil.

When Africans met with indigenous people inside the Brazilian forests, a recognition and identification of how similar their religious conceptions were. A ancestry, the forces of nature as the main rulers of life on earth, the principle of energy as a fundamental factor of spirituality, the presence of the invisible, harmony and integration between the spiritual and material worlds. All these common conceptions between what already existed in America and what was brought by Africans were perceived in the contact between such peoples. From the social relations that emerged within the quilombos and in the contact between quilombolas and indigenous people there was a continuous integration between religious rituals and knowledge accumulated by both parties.

Over time, with the configurations and formation of the terreiros of Catimbó, Jurema, Terecô, Xangô, Amolokô, Candomblé, Umbanda and others, the caboclos became primordial entities In these cults, it becomes almost impossible to distinguish between what is African and what is He is indigenous to this cult of caboclos.

There were also other measures, the Christianization of indigenous peoples. Brazilian Indians, For the most part, they accepted Christian doctrine very well as a religious reference. The principle of charity, forgiveness, unconditional love for others, doing good without seeing who, were principles to which indigenous people easily identified, especially on the part of Pajés and Caciques who were responsible for the political and social organization of the communities indigenous people.

Throughout the process of religious syncretization, that is, the mixing of religious traditions, the participation of the indigenous tradition, essentially Brazilian, took place in the figure of the caboclo. Or that is, among all the religions and manifestations existing in Brazil, the contribution and representation of what is really ours, are in Caboclos.
