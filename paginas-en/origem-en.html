Origin of indigenous in Brazil and America: How it was, theories

When European colonizers arrived in America, it was already populated. There is a big discussion about the way in which man managed to reach the continent, considering that there was no connection between it and another continent through emerging lands.

Currently, there are two theories that try to explain the arrival of man on the American continent: the transoceanic theory and the Bering theory. According to transoceanic theory, there are around 10 thousand years, the men who inhabited Polynesia (in the Oceania region) moved towards South America in small boats. These would have moved through sea currents that led them.

According to Bering's theory, man arrived in America through the Bering Strait, located between the extreme east of the Asian continent and the extreme west of the continent American, the two points are 85 km apart. According to this theory, the arrival of man to the American continent occurred approximately 50 thousand years ago, when Asian nomads crossed the Bering Strait; which in that period was frozen due to the era glacial, thus forming a natural bridge between the two points. From there, man migrated to the southern part of the American continent. These are theories that have greater acceptability in the scientific world, but there is no certainty about his statements.

In the context of Brazilian territory, the most precise evidence indicates that human presence dates back 12 thousand years before the present. This penetration, according to geographer Aziz Nacib Ab’Sáber, happened in the Amazon, as the region had vast corridors of semi-arid climate, which facilitated movement.

However, one discovery challenges researchers. There are archaeological remains that point to of the possibility of human presence in the region of current Piauí around 43 thousand years before the gift. However, researchers have no other evidence to support this fact..
