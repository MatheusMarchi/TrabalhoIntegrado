Struggles of Indigenous peoples: Date and purposes

The National Day of Struggle of Indigenous Peoples, celebrated on February 7th, reinforces the fight for right to land, against the destruction of nature, for the preservation of culture and traditions and for demarcation of land of original peoples.

The date was chosen in honor of the Guarani Sepé Tiaraju, who died on February 7, 1756, during the battle of Caiboaté, in which around 1500 indigenous warriors were killed after being expelled from their lands, due to the Treaty of Madrid – which decreed the evacuation of the lands indigenous people.

In order to represent this struggle of more than 250 years, seeking and demanding solutions, the people indigenous people expanded their presence on the political scene. In 2022, 186 applications were launched indigenous people, compared to 133 in 2018 and 85 in 2014. In the Chamber of Deputies, five candidates indigenous people were elected:

  • Sônia Guajajara (PSOL-SP)
  • Célia Xakriabá (PSOL-MG)
  • Juliana Cardoso (PT-SP)
  • Paulo Guedes (PT-MG)
  • Silvia Waiãpi (PL-AP)

With lands being invaded, indigenous peoples and traditional communities have been fighting and charging public bodies for land demarcation and more rigorous search measures, seizure and punishment of invaders.

In 2021, 305 incidents of invasions, illegal exploitation and damage to heritage in 226 indigenous lands, according to the Violence Against Peoples report Indigenous People of Brazil, published by the Indigenous Missionary Council (Cimi). These crimes are directly linked to illegal mining, land grabbing, hunting in the territories, illegal exploitation of wood and fishing.
